Measuring Heart Signals

The first question about what this project is may come from our our title, That being what exactly our project wants to do with cardiac signals. Well Its fairly simple, we want to implement code that uses the fast fourier transform and the short time fourier transform to take data and convert it into visible signals. From there, we may will attempt to make code that can take those methods and use them to find heart diseases from those signals.

Parts of the Project

  • Fast Fourier Transform

    The first and most commonly-used method of cardiac signal processing used in hospitals.

  • Short Time Fourier Transform

    The faster and more mobile method used for cardiac signal prcoessing (usually in the field).

  • Arrythmia Detection

    On top of these two parts of the project, we also plan on eriting code that can detect heart diseases such as arrythmia

  • Faster Than Fast Fourier Transform

    An experimental method that uses various algorithms and optimizations to be faster than the prior two - This one may or may not be utilized in our project.

Project Roadmap

Like all projects, this one isn't yet done.
Here are the the milestones and our progress so far.

  • Research and Planning 100% Complete
  • Fast Fourier Transform 95% Complete
  • Short Time Fourier Transform 95% Complete
  • Arrythmia Detector Code 5% Complete
  • Final App and Website 15% Complete

Our Project has gone through quite a few rewrites and scope refocuses. This has come down to a variety of reasons, but perhaps most prominently, the recent COVID-19 outbreak; for this reason, we were actually forced to rollback our scope (which originally covered the Faster-than-fast Fourier transform as well) to only cover the FFT and STFT. Additonally, other changes (such as a rollback on the diseases detector)also have occurred in our plans, producing this roadmap as a result.

Current Progress

As shown above in the Milestones tab, Our project is progressing fairly well, the problem however, has arised in how exactly we will move forward. Seeing as we no longer have the ability to obtain real life samples of heart rates for our application to process for the final part of the project, we've been forced to reconsider how we want to go about obtaining the data that we need.
Much of this has come in the form of us "reterofitting our project to work exclusively with data from online cardiac signal providers (such as the American Heart Association)". We've also made strides in attempting to raise our quality of work, seeing as we will no longer be able to achieve our previous project scope.
